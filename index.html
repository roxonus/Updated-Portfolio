About Me

I was born in 1981 in a small town in upstate New York called New Lebanon. When I say small town I mean it. My high school was grade 7 to grade 12 and there were only 350 kids in the school. I graduated from high school and spent a couple years in community college studying moslty the arts as I always wanted to be an actor growing up. At 18 I started a band and I never looked back.

That band was called Hed 1st and we played for about 6 years all over New York State and Massachusetts. I got a job as a masons tender helping build brick houses, wood burning masonry firplaces and stone walls and patios. This became my trade that I would continue to practice for the next 20 years.

The band broke up and I moved to Chicago to start a new band with some friends I met while playing with touring bands in New York. I got a job in Chicago geared more toward masonry restoration instead of custom residential masonry. The band I put together in chicago was called Vile Display of Humanity and I would continue to create music with this band for the next 10 years. After 2 years in Chicago we decided to pack up and move to Seattle together in 2009.

I continued to work as a mason while playing music with VDH from 2009 until the band broke up in 2017. I decided that playing in a band was something that I couldn't continue to dedicate so much time to after my son, Logan was born in 2014. I found new passion in renewing my love for fishing and teaching myself to learn to ice skate. I honed my skating skills so that I could sign up to play Hockey which was always a love of mine but never had the guts to try. In 2018 I became a right wing, number 22 on the Seattle Stingers in the Greater Seattle Hockey League. I've scored 7 goals and 17 assists in 67 games with the Stingers.

I decided to apply for the University of Washington Fullstack Coding Bootcamp in 2020 because construction seemed to be a dead end career path with very limited options. I would like to become a web developer and prove that I can be as successful with my brain as I am with my body.
